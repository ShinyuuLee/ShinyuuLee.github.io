Style Transfer

Dec 15, 2017

Have you ever been drawn to Van Gogh’s unique style? Visit Loving Vincent which collects 65,000 Van Gogh style paintings drawn by 115 professional artists and enjoy it. Admittedly, to adjust a picture into this style may require a lot of professional skills. So here comes the question. Could we let computers replace us to achieve style transfer automatically without any expertise?

This essay will introduce how to make computer learn style transfer through CNN. In fact, style transfer is often encountered in our lives, such as filters. I remember there was once a popular film called who are you(中文:你的名字), and at that time some image processing apps provided the functions to turn photos into the film style ones to attract users. This is a quite interesting and useful model which teachs computers to do art creation.

Gatys etc. introduce A Neural Algorithm of Artistic Style that can separate and recombine the image content and style of natural images, and this algorithm allows us to transfer the style from one image onto another.

We first pick a convolution neural network to extract features. We choose three specific layers to match the style and a specific layer to match the content. Then we give the style and content images and save the style and content layer outputs $s$ and $c$ respectively. After that, we constantly optimize the value $x$ of the initial image by minimizing the style loss & content loss between features extracted from $x$ and the fixed outputs $s,c$.

Suppose $y{i}$ represents 2D ndarray output of layer $i$ with two dimentions channel number and height × width. And we also resize $ s{i},c{i} $ of neural network layer $ i $ to keep same shape with $ y{i} $.

The content loss function uses the MSE (mean square error).
$$ \textrm{ContentLoss}(c_i, y_i) =\frac{ | c_i - y_i |^2_F}{channel \times height \times width} $$

For style match, a basic idea is to match the statistical distribution. Matching statistical distribution commonly used approach is momentum matching. Here we only consider the momentum up to second order. So we only need to consider the difference between covariance matrices on the assumption that means are zero.
$$ \textrm{StyleLoss}(s_i, y_i) = \frac{| s_i s_i^T - y_i y_i^T |^2_F}{channel^2 \times height \times width} $$

Here we also have to apply Total variation denoising which has applications in noise removal. Otherwise, we can observe a lot of high-frequency noises in the pictures we learned. Suppose $x_{i,j}$ represents image pixel $(i,j)$.

$$ \textrm{TvLoss}(x{ij}) = \sum{i,j} |x{i,j} - x{i+1,j}| + |x{i,j} - x{i,j+1}| $$
